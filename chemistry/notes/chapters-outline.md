### Key Points of Chapter 1
- Chemistry is the study of matter around us.
- Organic chemistry deals with carbon compounds (except bicarbonates, carbonates, oxides of carbon, and carbides).
- Inorganic chemistry deals with elements and their compounds (except organic compounds).
- Industrial chemistry is concerned with large-scale production of chemical substances.
- Physical chemistry deals with laws and theories to understand the structure and changes of matter.
- Science is defined as the study of nature.
- Technology is the application of science.

### Key Points of Chapter 2
- Matter has mass and occupies space.
- Plasma is an electrically charged gas affected by electrical and magnetic fields.
- Allotropy: an element exists in different physical forms.
- Element: simplest form of matter made of one type of atoms.
- Compound: formed when different atoms chemically combine.
- Solution: homogeneous mixture of two or more components.
- Aqueous solution: formed by dissolving a substance in water.
- Saturated solution: maximum solute at given temperature.
- Unsaturated solution: less solute than required to saturate at given temperature.
- Colloid: particles 1–1000 nm.
- Suspension: heterogeneous mixture; solid particles spread through liquid without dissolving.

### Key Points of Chapter 3
- Rutherford proposed a planetary model for an atom.
- Nucleus contains protons; electrons surround nucleus and occupy most volume.
- Bohr model: electrons revolve in fixed circular orbits (shells).
- Isotopes differ in number of neutrons.
- ²³⁵₉₂U is used in nuclear reactors and atomic bombs.
- Radioisotopes are used in medicine and science (cancer treatment, thyroid diagnosis, imaging, detecting obstructions, dating, etc.).
- Shells are divided into subshells: s, p, d, f.
- Arrangement of electrons in subshells is electronic configuration.
- Aufbau principle: electrons fill lowest energy levels first.
